What is Golf Ring Master?

The Golf Ringmaster is your personal caddie on and around the green.

Designed in Australia and made from high quality stainless steel, your Golf Ringmaster comes with a lifetime Guarantee, meaning that it will be your lifelong golfing buddy.

Design

The Golf Ringmaster has very unique and top quality design.

With its angled body, the Golf Ringmaster can be easily and repeatedly inserted and removed from any of your putters without ever unintentionally coming off.

How To Use

Simply insert the spiked end of your Ringmaster into the end of your putter and push home until the ring is against the end of the handle.



Using the ring end of the Ringmaster, trap the ball against the cup and scoop it up right into your hand. If you like you can even use your foot to keep the ball still while scooping it up with the ring.

Health

Studies have shown that golf is good for your health and safe for your heart. These benefits don’t come from swinging your club but from walking. In fact, walking 18 holes corresponds to up to four miles. Playing golf three times a week will give your heart a good amount of endurance exercise, and if you. are pulling or carrying your clubs you will be burning even more calories.

However, it is no secret that golf is a rough sport for your body. Years of swinging and hitting the ball can lead to a great amount of wear and tear on your back and knees.

Harvard Medical School

According to Harvard Medical School, since a golf swing involves the whole body, any part of the body can be injured while playing.

The most common injuries include back muscle strain, shoulder problems and hand and wrist injuries. Some players can also experience serious back injuries. To see the suggestions the Harvard Medical School have for avoiding injuries

Advice

The Harvard Medical School offers some advice for avoiding injuries:

Stretch at least three times a week. Warm-up for 10-15 minutes before you play. Consider taking lessons to improve your technique. Don’t ignore early strain signs. Use ice on aching tissues after playing and use the PRICE (Protection, Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation) approach to treat more serious problems. When in doubt, always seek the advice of your medical practitioner.
